Project Overview

This Wigston homeowner had a 28m² timber deck that had been in place for over 10 years. Despite being treated annually, the boards had developed significant rot, several were soft underfoot, and the whole structure had begun to bow away from the house. It was no longer safe.

Rather than replacing like-for-like with timber and facing the same maintenance cycle in another decade, the customer chose composite decking — specifically a capped composite board in slate grey that would complement their grey rendered extension and existing porcelain patio below.

The project also included a new stainless steel and glass balustrade to replace the original painted timber posts, and integrated LED post cap lights to extend the use of the deck into evenings.

Materials Used

Capped Composite Boards — Slate Grey Pressure-Treated C16 Timber Subframe Hidden Clip Fixing System Composite Fascia Boards Stainless Steel Balustrade Post Cap Lights

Scope of Work

  • Full removal and disposal of existing timber deck
  • New pressure-treated timber subframe on adjustable feet
  • 28m² capped composite board installation
  • Hidden clip fixing system — no visible screws
  • Composite fascia and trim to all edges
  • Stainless steel balustrade with glass infill panels
  • Integrated post cap LED lighting

How We Did It

A step-by-step look at the transformation process.

1

Design & Specification

The customer chose their board colour and style from our sample range during the survey visit. Board orientation was agreed to run away from the house for maximum visual length.

2

Deck Removal

The existing timber deck and all supporting posts were carefully removed and disposed of. The ground beneath was inspected, levelled and a weed membrane laid.

3

Subframe Construction

A new pressure-treated C16 timber subframe was installed on adjustable feet at 400mm joist centres, set to the correct falls for drainage and perfectly level across the full width.

4

Board Installation

Capped composite boards were installed using a hidden clip system — no visible screws anywhere on the deck surface. Ends were capped with matching composite fascia.

5

Balustrade & Lighting

The stainless steel balustrade with toughened glass infill panels was installed to three sides of the deck. LED post cap lights were fitted and wired to an outdoor timer socket.

6

Final Handover

The completed deck was inspected, cleaned and handed over with the composite board care guide and the 25-year manufacturer board warranty.

The Results

The Wigston homeowner described the result as 'a completely different garden'. The combination of the slate grey composite deck and glass balustrade has transformed what was a tired, unsafe structure into a premium outdoor entertaining space.

The integrated lighting means the deck is used well into the evening throughout summer, and the homeowner noted that the space now genuinely functions as an extension of their living room.

Project completed in 3 days — on budget and on time. 5-star Google review received the following week.
