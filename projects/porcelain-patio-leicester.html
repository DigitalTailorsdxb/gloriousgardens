Project Overview

This Leicester homeowner in the LE3 postcode had lived with a cracked, stained concrete slab patio for over 12 years. The surface had heaved in several places due to frost damage, and moss and weeds had taken hold in every joint. It was unsafe, unsightly, and simply wasn't being used.

The brief was clear: create a premium outdoor entertaining space that would complement the rear extension they'd recently had built. The customer had seen large-format porcelain patios online and wanted a clean, contemporary look that would be easy to maintain.

We specified 900×600mm light grey porcelain slabs in a staggered bond pattern — a format that creates an impressive, open feel and works beautifully against the grey render of their extension. The entire project was completed in 4 working days.

Materials Used

900×600mm Porcelain Slabs Grey Limestone Edging Polymeric Jointing Compound MOT Type 1 Sub-base Compacted Hardcore

Scope of Work

  • Full removal of existing concrete slab and disposal
  • Excavation to 250mm depth
  • Compacted MOT Type 1 sub-base installation
  • Large-format 900×600mm porcelain laying
  • Flexible pointing with weather-resistant compound
  • Limestone edging and step construction
  • Full site clearance and clean-up

How We Did It

A step-by-step look at the transformation process.

1

Site Survey & Design

We visited the property to take precise measurements, assess drainage falls, and confirm the porcelain specification. The customer had used our free AI design tool to get a preview before booking.

2

Excavation & Clearance

The existing concrete slab was broken up and removed, along with the original sub-base. All waste was taken away from site. Ground was excavated to 250mm below finished patio level.

3

Sub-Base Construction

150mm of MOT Type 1 aggregate was laid and compacted in two passes using a vibrating plate compactor. Falls of 1:80 were established away from the property for surface water drainage.

4

Porcelain Laying

A full mortar bed was screeded and each 900×600mm porcelain slab was laid individually, checked for level and fall at every course. Spacers maintained consistent 5mm joints throughout.

5

Pointing & Finishing

Joints were filled with a light grey flexible pointing compound and finished flush. Limestone edging courses were installed to all perimeter edges. The patio was cleaned down and sealed.

6

Handover

The finished patio was walked through with the customer, including care instructions. The project completed 1 day ahead of schedule.

The Results

The transformation exceeded the customer's expectations. What was once a neglected and dangerous concrete slab is now a premium outdoor living space that flows seamlessly from the rear extension doors.

The large-format porcelain gives the garden a sense of space and luxury that has genuinely changed how the family uses their garden. The customer noted that they now use the space daily during warmer months — something they never did before.

The project was completed on budget, within 4 days, and received a 5-star review on Google.
