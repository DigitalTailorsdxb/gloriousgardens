Project Overview

This Oadby homeowner came to us with an 80m² rear garden that had been neglected for several years following a house purchase. The garden was overgrown, the existing shed was beyond repair, the lawn was more weed than grass, and there was no coherent design to the space at all.

The brief was comprehensive: create a garden that could be used for family entertaining, was safe for children, had some growing space, was easy to maintain, and looked beautiful. The budget of £18,500 was agreed upfront, and we designed the space to maximise value within it.

The design used our AI visualisation tool as a starting point, which the customer refined through two rounds of feedback before we produced final working drawings. The result is a garden that functions beautifully across multiple zones — dining patio, lawn, growing beds and boundaries.

Materials Used

600×600mm Porcelain Paving 35mm Artificial Turf Composite Raised Beds LED Garden Lighting System Painted Timber Fencing Oak Railway Sleepers Block Paving Path

Scope of Work

  • Full garden clearance — existing lawn, borders and shed removed
  • Groundworks including levelling and drainage installation
  • 40m² porcelain patio with step and edging detail
  • 30m² artificial grass with shaped edges to curved borders
  • Three composite raised vegetable beds with sleeper edging
  • New painted close-board fencing to three boundaries
  • 12-light LED garden lighting system with smart control
  • Block paving side path to gate

How We Did It

A step-by-step look at the transformation process.

1

AI Design & Planning

We started with an AI-powered design preview, then developed full CAD drawings showing every element. The customer approved the design after two rounds of minor revisions.

2

Full Clearance

Everything existing was removed — lawn, borders, shed, old fencing and the original broken concrete path. The site was left as a blank canvas for the new design.

3

Groundworks & Drainage

Ground levels were established across the full garden. A new linear drainage channel was installed across the patio threshold, and the lawn area was graded to drain to a soakaway.

4

Hard Landscaping

The porcelain patio was laid first, followed by the block paving side path and the raised bed edging. All boundaries were fenced before soft landscaping began.

5

Soft Landscaping & Lighting

The artificial grass was installed to the lawn zone, the raised beds were constructed and filled, and the 12-light LED system was installed and commissioned.

6

Snagging & Handover

A full snagging inspection was carried out on day 12. Minor adjustments were made, and the garden was cleaned and handed over with all warranties and care guides.

The Results

The Oadby homeowner described the transformation as 'life-changing' — and we understand exactly what they mean. An overgrown, unusable space is now a beautifully designed garden that the whole family uses every day.

The project was delivered on time and on the agreed budget of £18,500. The customer has since referred two neighbours to us — the highest compliment we can receive.

The garden features across every zone: the porcelain patio for dining, the artificial lawn for the children, the raised beds for growing, and the lighting system that brings it all to life in the evenings.
