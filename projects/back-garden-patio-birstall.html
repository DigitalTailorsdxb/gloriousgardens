Project Overview

This Birstall homeowner had a 40m² rear garden that was almost entirely covered in an old, heavily cracked concrete slab. The surface had been laid decades ago without adequate falls, meaning puddles formed after every shower of rain. There were no planting areas, no defined zones — just a flat grey expanse.

The customer wanted a warm, characterful outdoor space that felt inviting and personal. They were drawn to the natural variation and warm tones of Indian sandstone, and wanted some raised planting built into the design to bring greenery and structure to the space.

We designed a scheme that uses buff multi Indian sandstone across the main patio area, with two raised planters built in sandstone-faced walling positioned to frame the view from the kitchen doors. LED spotlights installed into the planters and at ground level complete the scheme, creating a space that looks equally beautiful by day and night.

How We Did It

A step-by-step look at the transformation process.

1

Design & Quote

The customer used our free AI quote tool to get a preview of the Indian sandstone scheme. The design was finalised with the addition of the two raised planters following the site survey.

2

Concrete Removal

The old concrete slab was broken up using a breaker and removed from site along with the original sub-base. Ground conditions were assessed and drainage falls planned.

3

Sub-Base & Drainage

150mm compacted MOT Type 1 sub-base was installed across the full area with 1:80 falls to a new gully at the corner of the patio. Edge restraints were installed.

4

Sandstone Laying

Buff multi Indian sandstone was laid in a random pattern with consistent 10mm joints. The raised planters were constructed in sandstone-faced blockwork simultaneously.

5

Lighting & Pointing

Six LED spotlights were installed — two within the raised planters and four as ground-level path lights. All patio joints were pointed with a buff flexible compound and the surface sealed.

6

Planting & Handover

Decorative gravel was laid to the planting pockets at the base of the planters. The customer's own plants were positioned in the raised beds as part of the handover.

The Results

The Birstall patio transformation shows what's possible even with a relatively modest budget and a limited footprint. The warm tones of the Indian sandstone and the built-in raised planters have completely changed the character of the space.

The homeowner commented that the garden now feels like a proper outdoor room — somewhere they genuinely choose to spend time, rather than a space they look at through the window and ignore.
