Project Overview

First impressions matter — and this Wigston homeowner knew their front garden was letting down an otherwise well-maintained property. The existing front garden was a mix of cracked tarmac, an overgrown lawn strip, and a tired concrete path. The boundary walls were crumbling and the whole frontage lacked any coherent design.

The brief was to create a front garden that added real kerb appeal, provided practical off-street parking for two cars, and was completely low-maintenance. The homeowner also wanted the entrance to feel welcoming and characterful.

We designed a scheme using charcoal block paving for the driveway and path, flanked by resin gravel borders with evergreen planting. Rebuilt boundary walls with natural stone pier caps and integrated LED pillar lights complete the entrance statement.

Materials Used

Charcoal Block Paving Natural Stone Pier Caps Brick Boundary Wall Resin Gravel Border Low-Maintenance Planting LED Pillar Lights Steel Edging

Scope of Work

  • Removal of existing tired lawn and concrete path
  • New block paving driveway and footpath
  • Brick boundary wall rebuild with stone coping
  • Resin gravel planting borders either side of entrance
  • Low-maintenance evergreen planting scheme
  • Two LED pillar lights to entrance piers
  • New steel edging throughout

How We Did It

A step-by-step look at the transformation process.

1

Design & Planning

A full measured survey was carried out. The design balanced the practical requirement for two parking spaces with an attractive planted entrance scheme.

2

Clearance & Excavation

Existing tarmac, lawn, path and crumbling wall sections were removed. Ground was excavated to 200mm below finished driveway level throughout.

3

Sub-Base & Drainage

150mm compacted MOT Type 1 sub-base was laid across the full driveway area. Drainage falls were established and a gully fitted at the entrance to intercept surface water.

4

Block Paving & Walls

Charcoal block paving was laid in a herringbone pattern. Boundary walls were rebuilt in matching brick with natural limestone coping and pier caps.

5

Planting, Gravel & Lights

Resin gravel borders were formed with steel edging. Evergreen shrubs and ornamental grasses were planted. LED pillar lights were installed to both entrance piers.

6

Jointing & Handover

Polymeric jointing sand was brushed into all block paving joints and compacted. The full frontage was cleaned down and handed over.

The Results

The transformation of this Wigston frontage is dramatic. What was a tired, uninviting approach is now a polished, well-designed entrance that draws compliments from neighbours and passers-by alike.

The homeowner noted an immediate improvement in how the property looks from the street, and commented that the LED pillar lights in particular make a huge difference in the evenings.

Project completed in 5 days at £7,800. The homeowner subsequently commissioned us for a rear garden redesign — a strong endorsement of the quality of the front garden work.
