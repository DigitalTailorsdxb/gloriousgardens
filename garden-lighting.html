Why Garden Lighting Transforms Leicester Gardens

Leicester's latitude means we lose outdoor daylight from around 4pm in midwinter. Well-designed garden lighting extends your enjoyment of the garden by months each year — turning an unusable dark space into an inviting outdoor room you can use from the evening meal through to late autumn.

Beyond lifestyle, garden lighting has a measurable impact on security and property value. A well-lit property is significantly less likely to be targeted by opportunist burglars, and buyers consistently rate quality outdoor lighting as one of the features that make a property feel premium and well-cared-for.

We design and install low-voltage LED garden lighting systems across Farnborough and Leicestershire — from subtle path lighting and uplighting on specimen trees to fully zoned smart systems controlled from your phone. Every installation is designed with a proper electrical specification before a cable is laid.

What to Expect on Garden Lighting Installation Day

1

Design Walk-Through

We start with a site walk to confirm fixture positions, cable routes and transformer location. If combining with other landscaping, lighting cables are always laid before surfaces go down.

2

Cable Laying

Low-voltage cable is laid in conduit at the correct burial depth, routed to avoid plant root zones where possible. All cable joins are fully weatherproof.

3

Fixture Installation

Fittings are installed and connected. We check IP ratings for each position — anything near water features needs IP68 rated fittings.

4

Transformer & Control Setup

The transformer is installed, timer and dusk-to-dawn sensor configured, and smart controls set up if included.

5

Testing & Handover

Every fitting is tested, beam angles adjusted, and we walk you through the control system with a simple zone guide.

Common Garden Lighting Mistakes to Avoid

Wrong IP rating for the location

IP44 fittings are not suitable near ponds or anywhere subject to water submersion. Always check the IP rating matches the installation environment — we specify this correctly for every fixture.

Choosing the wrong colour temperature

Warm white (2700–3000K) creates an inviting atmosphere. Cool white (5000K+) looks clinical and harsh outdoors. Most Leicester garden projects use warm white throughout.

No circuit zoning

A single on/off switch for the whole garden is a missed opportunity. Zoning the system lets you set the right mood for different uses and reduces electricity costs.

Surface-mounting cables rather than burying

Cable clipped along fences or over surfaces is an eyesore and a trip hazard. All permanent garden lighting cables should be correctly buried or housed in conduit.
