Granite Patios Farnborough — Frequently Asked Questions

Granite is significantly harder to quarry and cut, which increases material costs. However, its superior durability means lower long-term maintenance costs — most granite patios never need replacing.
Granite is very dense with extremely low porosity — it can be left unsealed outdoors. However, applying a sealant every 5–10 years further improves stain resistance, particularly around BBQ or dining areas.
Polished granite can be very slippery when wet. We exclusively use flamed, sandblasted or bush-hammered finishes for outdoor applications to ensure safe grip in all weather conditions.
Granite has extremely low water absorption, making it completely frost-proof. Unlike some natural stones, it will not crack or spall in freezing conditions — even without sealing.
Granite is essentially permanent — the stone itself will outlast any building. With correct installation, a granite patio will still look excellent in 50–100 years.
Yes — granite setts (cobbles) and large format slabs are excellent for driveways too. Using the same stone across both areas creates a beautiful, cohesive look for the front and rear of your property.
