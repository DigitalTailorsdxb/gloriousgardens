Garden Walls Leicester — Frequently Asked Questions

Walls under 1m adjacent to a highway or under 2m elsewhere generally don't require planning permission. We advise on this at the survey and will flag any requirements before work begins.
A standard brick boundary wall takes 1–3 days per 10 linear metres depending on height and complexity. Natural stone walling takes longer due to the skilled work involved in fitting irregular stones.
Foundation depth depends on wall height, soil type and drainage. As a guide, a 1m-high wall needs foundations of at least 450mm depth. We always excavate to the appropriate depth for the specific soil conditions.
Yes — we use stepped foundations to accommodate slopes. Stepped or battered wall designs can also be used to follow ground contours in a visually attractive way.
Brick is the most versatile and cost-effective for most Leicester gardens. Natural stone gives the best long-term appearance. Rendered blockwork is most economical for longer runs.
We work with specialist brick suppliers and reclaim yards to achieve the closest possible match to existing brickwork. Exact matches aren't always possible with older properties but we always get as close as we can.
