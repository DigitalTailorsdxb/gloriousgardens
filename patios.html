Why Farnborough Homeowners Choose a Patio

Farnborough gardens tend to be generous in size but often underused — especially when lawns turn to mud every winter. A well-built patio changes that immediately. It gives you a dry, usable outdoor space from March through to November, and with the right materials it requires almost no maintenance year-on-year.

Hampshire's clay-heavy soil can be particularly unforgiving on cheap patio installations. We always excavate to a proper depth and lay a compacted sub-base before any slabs go down — this prevents the settling, cracking and rocking that affects DIY jobs and budget installs within a few years.

From the period homes in Stoneygate to the newer builds in Kirby Muxloe and Oadby, we've laid patios across Farnborough's neighbourhoods and understand the unique ground conditions, planning constraints and garden styles across the area.

What to Expect on Patio Installation Day

1

Morning: Access & Mark-Out

Our team arrives and marks out the patio area to the agreed design. Any furniture or plants in the way are moved carefully. We lay protection sheets over existing lawns and borders.

2

Excavation & Sub-Base

We excavate to the correct depth — typically 150–200mm — and lay a compacted MOT Type 1 sub-base. This is the most important part of the job; get it wrong and the patio will fail regardless of how good the slabs are.

3

Sand Bed & Laying

A screed bed of sharp sand and cement is prepared, and slabs are laid to your agreed pattern. Each slab is checked for level as it goes down.

4

Pointing & Finishing

Once laid, joints are filled with a flexible jointing compound suitable for UK weather. The finished patio is brushed clean and inspected.

5

Handover

We walk you through maintenance advice, remove all waste, and leave your garden cleaner than we found it. Most standard patios are ready to use within 24–48 hours.

Common Patio Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the sub-base

The most expensive mistake. Without a proper compacted sub-base, slabs will rock, crack or sink within 2–3 years — especially on Hampshire's clay soil.

Choosing the cheapest slab

Budget paving often looks great on day one but fades, chips or absorbs water. Porcelain is more expensive upfront but lasts 25+ years with virtually zero maintenance.

No drainage gradient

A patio that doesn't slope slightly away from the house will puddle. We build a 1-in-80 gradient into every installation as standard.

DIY pointing with standard cement

Ordinary cement cracks during freeze-thaw cycles. Always use a flexible, weather-resistant pointing compound designed for outdoor paving.
