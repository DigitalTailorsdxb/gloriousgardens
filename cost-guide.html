Patio installation costs in the UK vary significantly depending on the material you choose, the size of the area and the complexity of the groundworks required. Here are typical price ranges for a standard 20–40m² patio in 2026.

Porcelain tiles (20mm)£60–£120/m²
Natural Indian sandstone£45–£90/m²
Granite setts / cobbles£80–£140/m²
Block paving£50–£100/m²
Concrete / tarmac£30–£60/m²

Typical project totals (supply & install)

Small patio (15–25m²)£1,500–£3,500
Medium patio (25–45m²)£3,000–£6,500

These prices include excavation, disposal of excavated material, a compacted MOT Type 1 sub-base, sand or cement bed, and pointing. They exclude decorative borders, steps or retaining walls which are priced additionally.

Artificial Grass Installation Costs

Artificial grass prices have become much more competitive in recent years as the product has improved. The key cost factors are the quality of the turf chosen and whether existing lawn or groundworks need removing first.

Cost by grass grade (installed, per m²)

Entry-level (25–30mm)£25–£40/m²
Mid-range (30–37mm)£40–£60/m²
Premium (37–45mm, dual-tone)£60–£85/m²

Typical project totals

Small garden (20–35m²)£900–£2,500
Medium garden (35–60m²)£2,000–£4,500
Large garden (60m²+)£3,500–£7,000+

Prices include removal of existing lawn, weed membrane, compacted aggregate base layer, infill sand and nailing/gluing of joins. Quality artificial grass carries a 7–15 year manufacturer warranty.

Composite Decking Costs

Composite decking costs more upfront than timber but saves money over its lifetime — no annual treating, no rot, no splintering. The main variable is the brand and profile of composite board you choose.

Composite decking (installed, per m²)

Entry composite (grooved board)£90–£130/m²
Mid-range composite£130–£180/m²
Premium capped composite£180–£250/m²
Timber decking (for comparison)£60–£100/m²

Typical project totals

Small deck (15–25m²)£2,500–£5,000
Medium deck (25–40m²)£4,500–£9,000
Large / elevated deck (40m²+)£8,000–£18,000+

Prices include treated timber subframe, composite boards, hidden fixing clips, fascia boards and perimeter trim. Glass balustrades, privacy screens and integrated LED lighting are all available at additional cost.

Garden Lighting Costs

Garden lighting can be added to a new project or retrofitted to an existing garden. Professional LED systems are energy-efficient, long-lasting and completely controllable via smartphone. Prices vary based on the number of fittings and complexity of the cabling run.

Typical garden lighting costs

Basic spike light set (6–8 lights)£300–£700
Pathway / deck lighting (10–15 lights)£700–£1,500
Full system with transformer & control (15–25 lights)£1,500–£3,500
Premium smart-controlled system (25+ lights)£3,000–£6,000+

All professional installations use low-voltage (12V/24V) systems for safety, with IP-rated fittings suitable for outdoor use. Prices include cabling, transformer and installation. Smart app-controllable systems are available at the higher end of pricing.

What Affects the Cost of Landscaping?

Several factors push prices up or down outside of the headline material choice. Understanding these helps you set a realistic budget and avoid surprises.

Ground conditions

Rocky ground, very soft clay or contaminated soil all add to excavation time and disposal costs. A site visit before quoting ensures we account for this accurately.

Access

Tight side access, no parking nearby or restricted vehicle access increases labour time. Rear gardens with no direct access require manual barrow runs which add cost.

Waste removal

Old patio slabs, concrete, soil and rubble all need disposing of legally. Skip hire or specialist removal is included in our quotes but varies by project scale.

Project complexity

Curves, multiple levels, steps, retaining walls, drainage channels and intricate patterns all increase labour time compared to straightforward rectangular layouts.

Location

Labour rates vary across the UK. Our Leicester and Midlands rates are typically lower than London prices, reflecting local market conditions.

Season & lead time

Booking well in advance (particularly for spring/summer) avoids premium rates. We offer competitive off-peak pricing for autumn and winter projects where suitable.
