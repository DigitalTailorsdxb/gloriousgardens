Timber Decking Leicester — Frequently Asked Questions

Sweep and wash regularly to prevent moss and algae build-up. Apply a decking oil or stain every 1–2 years to protect the surface and maintain colour. Use a decking cleaner before re-oiling.
Pressure-treated pine lasts 10–15 years with maintenance. Hardwoods like balau can last 25–40 years. The lifespan depends heavily on the quality of treatment and ongoing care.
Untreated or algae-covered timber can be slippery. We recommend grooved decking boards for better grip and an annual application of anti-slip decking treatment in shaded or wet areas.
Yes — we can replace individual boards, joists and posts without necessarily rebuilding the entire deck. We assess the structure during the survey and advise on the most cost-effective approach.
Softwood (pine, larch, cedar) is lighter, easier to work with and lower cost but requires more regular maintenance. Hardwood (balau, ipe) is denser, more durable and needs less treatment but costs more upfront.
Ground-level decking under 300mm generally falls under permitted development. Raised decking near boundaries or over 300mm may need planning permission. We advise on this at the survey stage.
