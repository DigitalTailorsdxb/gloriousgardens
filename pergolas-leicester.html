Pergolas Leicester — Frequently Asked Questions

Most pergolas fall under permitted development provided they meet size and position criteria (not covering more than 50% of the garden, not forward of the principal elevation etc.). We advise on this at survey stage.
A pergola is an open-sided structure with an open or slatted roof — ideal for climbing plants or partial shade. A gazebo is a fully enclosed, roofed structure. We build both.
A standard timber pergola takes 1–3 days. A larger louvred aluminium structure with lighting and accessories may take 3–5 days.
Yes — a lean-to or attached pergola is one of the most popular options as it creates a seamless indoor-outdoor transition. We fix to the wall using appropriate fixings and flashing to ensure a watertight connection.
Apply a UV-protective wood stain or oil every 1–2 years to maintain appearance and protect against weathering. Pressure-treated timber is resistant to rot but benefits from a decorative topcoat.
Yes — we can retrofit polycarbonate, glass, felt or GRP roofing panels to existing pergola structures. We assess the existing frame during the survey to confirm suitability.
