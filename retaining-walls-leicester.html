Retaining Walls Leicester — Frequently Asked Questions

Retaining walls under 1m adjacent to a highway or under 2m elsewhere generally don't require planning permission. Larger walls or those on sloped sites near boundaries may need a structural engineer's sign-off.
Foundation depth depends on wall height and soil conditions. As a guide, allow one-third of the total wall height below ground. Clay soils in Leicestershire may require deeper foundations to prevent movement.
All retaining walls must have adequate drainage to prevent water pressure building up behind the wall. We install a drainage membrane, gravel backfill and weepholes as standard on all retaining wall projects.
Railway sleeper walls are typically suitable for height changes up to 900mm–1200mm. For larger level changes, a concrete block or natural stone wall with proper structural foundations is more appropriate.
A retaining wall combined with proper drainage can significantly improve a waterlogged garden by managing water run-off and creating level terraces. We often pair retaining walls with French drains and soakaways.
A properly built concrete block or brick retaining wall with adequate drainage will last 30–50 years with minimal maintenance. The drainage is the most critical factor — a wall without drainage will fail far sooner.
