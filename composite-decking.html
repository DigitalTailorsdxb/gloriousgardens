Why Composite Beats Timber in the UK Climate

Traditional timber decking looks beautiful — for about two years. After that, the British climate takes over: green algae, grey weathering, warping, splinters and the annual ritual of sanding and re-oiling. Composite decking is engineered specifically to resist all of this, making it the smarter long-term choice for Leicester gardens.

Made from a blend of wood fibre and recycled plastic, composite boards hold their colour and structure through freeze-thaw cycles, summer heat, and the persistent moisture of a Midlands winter. Most premium composite ranges carry a 25-year structural warranty — something no timber board can match.

We install composite decking from trusted brands including Trex, Millboard, and Cladco. We'll help you choose the right range for your budget, garden style and usage — whether you want a natural wood effect for a traditional Leicester garden or a sleek contemporary finish.

What to Expect on Composite Decking Installation Day

1

Frame Construction

4

Final Inspection

We inspect the whole surface for level and secure fixing, then clean the deck down. Composite decking is ready to use immediately after installation.

Common Decking Mistakes to Avoid

Buying timber over composite

The upfront cost difference is real, but factor in 20+ years of annual oiling, sanding and potential board replacement and composite is significantly cheaper over a deck's lifetime.

Inadequate sub-frame spacing

Joists spaced too far apart cause boards to flex and feel bouncy underfoot. We follow manufacturer guidelines precisely — typically 400mm centres for composite boards.

No drainage gap under the deck

Decking built flush to the ground with no airflow underneath rots from the bottom up. We always maintain a 150mm+ clearance and ensure water can escape.

Wrong board direction

Boards should always run away from the house so water drains off the leading edge, not towards the wall. An obvious mistake that's costly to correct once the frame is built.
