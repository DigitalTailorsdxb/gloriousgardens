What Affects Patio Installation Cost?

📐 Size & Shape

Larger patios cost less per m² because fixed costs (groundworks, waste removal, labour setup) are spread over more area. Complex shapes with cuts add to labour cost.

🏗️ Ground Conditions

Soft, waterlogged or previously landscaped ground requires more excavation. Hard ground or areas with tree roots add to groundworks cost.

🧱 Sub-Base Depth

Clay soil (common in Leicestershire) requires a deeper sub-base to prevent movement. Standard depth is 100–150mm MOT Type 1; clay sites may need 200mm+.

🚛 Waste Removal

Removing an existing patio or concrete base adds £200–£800 to the cost depending on thickness and volume. This is included in our quotes as standard.

💧 Drainage

Adding drainage channels, soakaways or permeable jointing adds £150–£500 depending on complexity. Often worth it to prevent future water issues.

🪜 Steps & Features

Steps, raised edges, built-in planters and lighting are priced separately. Steps typically add £300–£600 per flight depending on material and height.
